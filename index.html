Research

My interests include developing mathematical structures for uncertainty, combinatorial optimization, stochastic optimization, and unified frameworks for nonlinear mathematical physics. I am also interested in topological applications for gradient-based optimization of physical systems such as agrivoltaics.

  • Nonlinear mathematical physics: I have developed a unified framework for physical systems such as fluid dynamics. Currently, I am translating this to a geometric framework where the physical systems appear as an effect of geodesic gradient flow.
  • Combinatorial optimization: This project bridges combinatorial optimization and modular forms. The idea is to define a seed function whose lifted form vanishes identically at the global optimum. Furthermore, I have developed a three-step Fourier, Hecke-relations, and completed L-function filter to test candidate tours for global optimality.
  • Stochastic optimization: This is an ongoing project where I use inverse Ricci flow to optimize stochastic problems.
  • Agrivoltaics: This is my Ph.D. work. I translated the problem of optimizing solar-panel tracking algorithms to finding the optimal random walk on the surface of a torus. It also includes chapters on data analysis, modeling, and optimization of agrivoltaic systems, in addition to using a hybrid LSTM-Transformer system for predicting plant yields.
